2/12 The Ultimate Bondage: Fear of Rejection

Most peoples decisions stem from the fear of isolation.

Isolation is a primordial fear. In ancient times, it would mean death.

So we cover our faces with powder. We say things we don’t mean. We do things we don’t want to do.

We do all this to feel accepted by the pack, for the alternative would be isolation and death.

But deep down, our true selves yearn to escape this bondage.
We yearn to feel connected to our bodies and our minds. The self, or the soul seeks authenticity.

What would you do if you weren’t afraid?

Would you would talk to that person, would you would stand up for yourself.
You would speak as a free man/woman, not as a slave to conformity.

How does one conquer this fear?
Fear grows in the darkness, it shrinks in the light.
When faced head on, it shrinks to the size of a pea.

Set small goals. Small victories accumulate to large victories.
Practice saying 'No'. Practice setting boundaries.
You will realise that doing so will ironically eliminate isolation.
Being serounded by people who respect your boundaries, the fake ones will scamper away.
