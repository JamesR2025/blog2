2/12 The Ultimate Bondage: Fear of Rejection

Most peoples decisions stem from the fear of isolation.

Isolation is a primordial fear. In ancient times, it would mean death.

So we cover our faces with powder. We say things we don’t mean. We do things we don’t want to do.

We do all this to feel accepted by the pack, for the alternative would be isolation and death.

But deep down, our true selves yearn to escape this bondage.
We yearn to feel connected to our bodies and our minds. The self, or the soul seeks authenticity.

What would you do if you weren’t afraid?

Would you would talk to that person, would you would stand up for yourself.
You would speak as a free man/woman, not as a slave to conformity.

How does one conquer this fear?
Fear grows in the darkness, it shrinks in the light.
When faced head on, it shrinks to the size of a pea.

Set small goals. Small victories accumulate to large victories.
Practice saying 'No'. Practice setting boundaries.
You will realise that doing so will ironically eliminate isolation.
Being serounded by people who respect your boundaries, the fake ones will scamper away.

2/13 Definition of Human: The animalistic

What does it mean to be human?

Despite our attempts to feel elevated from it, we are no different from the animals we look down upon.
We seek shelter, water and food to survive.
We seek safety in numbers.
We seek to provide value to the group, or risk being cast aside to die.
We seek partners who are strong and skilled hunters.
We seek partners who can bear and nourish children.
Ultimately, we are beings born with a self. We seek to maintain this self just as a knight defends a fortress.
We seek to conquer time by propagating this sense of self to our offspring, in the hope that our self lives on.
All human decisions revolve around self preservation.

2/13 Order and Chaos

In times of old, we were creatures at the mercy of nature.
We had very little control over plagues, war, and death.
So we invented stories, to create order from chaos.
Just as we glimpsed up at the stars and found patterns in the blackness.
Within ourselves there is a battle raging.
A battle between order and chaos.
When order rules, chaos begins to fester from within.
When chaos rules, there is a yearning for order.

Chaos comes in many different forms.
A feminine energy that grows and seeks to break free.
The fire within one's spirit to express, to fight through pain.
The indirect path which can wear down stone.
The intangible and the spiritual.
The spirit of man.

Order also emerges in different forms.
The masculine who creates laws and rulebooks.
The desire to feel protected and secure.
The desire to be direct and firm.
The worldly and the realistic.
The body of man.

The body contains the spirit, protecting it from the outside invaders.
The spirit guides the body, just as a captain guides a ship.
Each one holds just as much importance as the other.
They are one and the same.
In constant ebb and flow, like the tides of the sea.
Their battle appears much like a dance, and this battle appears in all things.
There is a battle within the individual,
A battle within individuals,
A battle within groups,
A battle within nations,
A battle within the world.
The way is not to control chaos and order, but to seek balance.
